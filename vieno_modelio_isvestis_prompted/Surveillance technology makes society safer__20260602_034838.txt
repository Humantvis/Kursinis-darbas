### Arguments in Favor:
1. **Crime Prevention:**
   - Surveillance cameras, facial recognition software, and other technologies can deter criminal activities by increasing the likelihood of detection.
   - Real-time monitoring allows authorities to respond quickly to incidents, potentially preventing crimes from occurring or reducing their severity.

2. **Public Safety:**
   - In areas with high crime rates, surveillance technology has been shown to reduce instances of theft, vandalism, and other offenses.
   - Enhanced security at public events, schools, and sensitive locations can protect people from harm.

3. **Efficient Law Enforcement:**
   - Surveillance aids in the swift identification and apprehension of criminals, leading to a more effective justice system.
   - It provides law enforcement with valuable evidence that helps solve cases more efficiently.

4. **Terrorism Prevention:**
   - Advanced surveillance systems can help track potential threats and prevent terrorist attacks by monitoring suspicious activities.
   - Data analysis tools can predict patterns of behavior associated with terrorism, enabling proactive measures.

5. **Public Accountability:**
   - Surveillance technology can also be used to monitor the actions of public officials, ensuring they adhere to ethical standards and laws.

### Arguments Against:
1. **Privacy Concerns:**
   - Extensive surveillance infringes on individual privacy rights, as it involves constant monitoring of people’s activities without their consent.
   - The data collected may be misused or fall into wrong hands, leading to potential abuse of personal information.

2. **Surveillance State:**
   - Over-reliance on surveillance can lead to a "surveillance state," where citizens are constantly monitored and controlled by authorities, undermining democratic principles.
   - It can create an environment of fear and distrust among the populace.

3. **False Positives and Bias:**
   - Surveillance systems may generate false positives, leading to wrongful accusations or harassment of innocent individuals.
   - Biases in algorithms used for facial recognition or predictive policing could disproportionately target certain groups based on race, gender, or socioeconomic status.

4. **Costs and Effectiveness:**
   - The implementation and maintenance of surveillance technology are often very expensive.
   - There is no conclusive evidence that increased surveillance directly correlates with reduced crime rates across all contexts.

5. **Chilling Effects on Free Speech and Dissent:**
   - Surveillance can discourage people from engaging in political activism or expressing dissenting opinions, stifling free speech and democratic discourse.

### Conclusion:
Surveillance technology has the potential to make society safer by preventing crimes, enhancing public safety, and aiding law enforcement. However, it also poses significant risks related to privacy, civil liberties, and the creation of a surveillance state. The effectiveness of such technologies in making society safer depends heavily on how they are implemented and regulated.

A balanced approach would involve:
- Ensuring that surveillance measures respect individual rights and freedoms.
- Implementing robust data protection laws to prevent misuse or unauthorized access to personal information.
- Regularly reviewing the impact of surveillance technology to ensure its benefits outweigh potential drawbacks.
- Promoting transparency in how surveillance systems are used, with clear guidelines on their application.

Ultimately, whether surveillance technology makes society safer depends on a careful balance between security and privacy, ethical considerations, and effective governance.
