\[ 10x = 9.999\ldots \]
Subtracting the first equation from the second, we get:
\[ 10x - x = 9.999\ldots - 0.999\ldots \]
\[ 9x = 9 \]
\[ x = 1 \]

### Proof by Limits:
Consider the infinite series representation of \(0.999\ldots\):
\[ 0.9 + 0.09 + 0.009 + \cdots \]
This is a geometric series with the first term \(a = 0.9\) and common ratio \(r = 0.1\). The sum of an infinite geometric series \(S = \frac{a}{1 - r}\) gives:
\[ S = \frac{0.9}{1 - 0.1} = \frac{0.9}{0.9} = 1 \]
