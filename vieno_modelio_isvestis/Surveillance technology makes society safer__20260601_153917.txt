The statement "Surveillance technology makes society safer" can be seen as both true and false, depending on how one interprets safety and considers the broader implications of surveillance.

**Arguments in Favor:**
1. **Crime Prevention:** Surveillance cameras, facial recognition software, and other monitoring technologies can deter criminal activity by increasing the likelihood that offenders will be caught.
2. **Law Enforcement Support:** Law enforcement agencies use surveillance to monitor suspicious activities, track down criminals, and solve crimes more efficiently.
3. **Public Spaces Safety:** In public areas like airports, stadiums, and city centers, surveillance systems help ensure that potential threats are identified early.

**Arguments Against:**
1. **Privacy Concerns:** Increased surveillance can lead to significant privacy invasions, as individuals may feel constantly monitored in their daily activities.
2. **Surveillance Overreach:** There is a risk of over-reliance on surveillance, which could result in the erosion of civil liberties and democratic norms.
3. **Bias and Misuse:** Surveillance systems can be prone to bias if not properly regulated, leading to disproportionate monitoring of certain groups or communities.
4. **False Sense of Security:** Relying heavily on surveillance might lead to complacency regarding other important safety measures, such as community engagement, education, and social support.

**Conclusion:**
While surveillance technology can contribute to making society safer in some contexts, it also introduces significant challenges related to privacy, civil liberties, and equity. The effectiveness of surveillance depends on how it is implemented, regulated, and balanced against individual rights. Therefore, the answer to whether "surveillance technology makes society safer" is nuanced and context-dependent.
