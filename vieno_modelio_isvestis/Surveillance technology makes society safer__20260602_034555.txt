The statement "Surveillance technology makes society safer" can be seen as both true and false, depending on how one interprets safety and the context in which surveillance is implemented. Here's a nuanced breakdown:

### Arguments for:
1. **Crime Prevention**: Surveillance cameras, facial recognition software, and other technologies can deter criminal activities by increasing the likelihood of being caught.
2. **Law Enforcement Support**: Police and security personnel can use surveillance data to solve crimes more efficiently, leading to faster resolutions and better public safety.
3. **Public Order Maintenance**: In public spaces like airports, train stations, or busy urban areas, surveillance can help maintain order and prevent potential threats.

### Arguments against:
1. **Privacy Concerns**: Surveillance technology often infringes on individual privacy rights. Constant monitoring can lead to a "Big Brother" scenario where personal freedoms are restricted.
2. **Misuse of Power**: There is always the risk that surveillance data could be misused or fall into the wrong hands, leading to abuse of power or invasion of privacy by authorities.
3. **Marginalization and Discrimination**: Surveillance systems can disproportionately target certain groups, exacerbating issues of racial profiling or discrimination.

### Conclusion:
Surveillance technology can contribute to making society safer in specific ways, but it also introduces significant risks related to privacy, civil liberties, and potential misuse. The effectiveness and ethical implications depend largely on how the technology is regulated, implemented, and balanced against individual rights.
